# Total Hip Arthroplasty in Patients with Proximal Femoral Migration

R analysis for a retrospective clinical study evaluating surgical outcomes of total hip
arthroplasty (THA) in patients with proximal femoral migration, assessing functional
recovery, anatomical correction, and pelvic realignment.

## Contents

- **Baseline Characteristics:** Demographic and clinical summary of the study cohort,
  reported as mean ± SD for continuous variables and n (%) for categorical variables.

- **Limb Length Discrepancy & Flexion Deformity:** Pre- and postoperative comparison of
  paired continuous outcomes reported as median (IQR) with 95% confidence intervals;
  statistical significance assessed using the Wilcoxon signed-rank test with continuity
  correction.

- **Harris Hip Score (Longitudinal):** Repeated measures of functional outcome across four
  time points (preoperative, 6 weeks, 6 months, 1 year), reported as mean ± SD and
  analyzed using one-way repeated-measures ANOVA.

- **Pairwise HHS Comparisons:** Post hoc pairwise comparisons of Harris Hip Score across
  all time-point combinations with Bonferroni correction, with adjusted p-values reported
  for each pair.

- **Pelvic Obliquity:** Pre- to postoperative change in coronal plane alignment assessed
  as a binary categorical outcome (present/absent) using McNemar's Chi-squared test with
  continuity correction.

- **Publication-Ready Outputs:** Tables formatted for manuscript submission (Tables 1–5);
  spaghetti plot of individual patient HHS trajectories overlaid with group mean and 95%
  confidence interval error bars across all follow-up time points (Figure 1).
